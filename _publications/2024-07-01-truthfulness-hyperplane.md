This paper presents a novel discovery: **a universal truthfulness hyperplane** exists within the internal representation space of large language models (LLMs). This hyperplane robustly separates truthful statements from untruthful ones across diverse topics, multiple model families, and different languages.

**Key contributions:**
- Discovery of a universal, transferable truthfulness direction in LLM hidden states
- Cross-model and cross-lingual generalization of the truthfulness hyperplane
- Probing classifiers trained on the hyperplane achieve state-of-the-art hallucination detection
- New interpretability insights into how LLMs internally represent truth vs. falsehood

Published at **EMNLP 2024**.

**Authors:** Junteng Liu, Shiqi Chen, Yu Cheng, Junxian He
