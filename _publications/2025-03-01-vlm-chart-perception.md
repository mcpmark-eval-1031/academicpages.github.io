This paper investigates why vision-language models (VLMs) struggle with **chart understanding** tasks. Through systematic analysis, we identify that the primary bottleneck lies in **low-level visual perception** — specifically, the inability to accurately extract numerical values and visual attributes from charts — rather than high-level reasoning failures.

**Key findings:**
- VLMs show significant perception bottlenecks in extracting visual data from charts
- Reasoning capability alone is insufficient without accurate perceptual grounding
- Introduced **Vision4Chart** benchmark for diagnosing perception vs. reasoning failures
- Proposed targeted interventions to improve chart understanding accuracy

**Authors:** Junteng Liu, Weihao Zeng, Xiwen Zhang, Yijun Wang, Zifei Shan, Junxian He
