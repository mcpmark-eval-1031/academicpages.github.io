We propose **SynLogic**, a scalable framework to synthesize verifiable logical reasoning data for training large language models (LLMs). Unlike existing approaches that rely on curated human-written data, SynLogic automatically generates diverse logic puzzles (spanning 35+ categories) with exact verifiable answers, enabling reinforcement learning from verifiable rewards (RLVR). Our approach dramatically expands the diversity and scale of reasoning training data while maintaining verifiability. Experiments demonstrate that SynLogic-trained models achieve strong logical reasoning performance and generalize well to out-of-distribution reasoning tasks.

**Key contributions:**
- A scalable data synthesis pipeline for 35+ types of logic puzzles with controllable difficulty
- Verifiable reward signals for RLVR training without human annotations
- Strong generalization from logical reasoning to broader reasoning benchmarks
